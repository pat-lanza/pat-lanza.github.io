Chipotle carries zero long-term debt and a net cash position of ~$351M. This is a significant credit strength — the clean balance sheet means 100% of firm value flows to equity holders and financial distress risk is negligible. That said, it also means there is no debt tax shield contributing to a lower WACC.

Key Takeaways
01

Significant Overvaluation Across All Scenarios

At $31.25, CMG trades above every methodology tested. Even the most optimistic scenario — bull case, exit multiple — tops out at $29.30, implying at minimum a 6% premium the market has baked in that fundamentals don't support.

02

High Terminal Value Dependency (75–78% of EV)

The vast majority of value is in the terminal value, not the explicit forecast period. This makes the valuation highly sensitive to assumptions about long-run growth and WACC — small changes in either produce large swings in implied price.

03

Revenue Growth Decelerating

After posting a 14% CAGR over the past decade, 2025 revenue growth slowed to just 5.4% — the weakest in the dataset. The market may be pricing in a reacceleration that our OLS model does not project without clear catalysts.

04

Pristine Balance Sheet — But No Debt Shield

Zero long-term debt and $350M in cash means negligible credit risk and maximum financial flexibility. However, the lack of leverage means Chipotle cannot lower its WACC through cheap debt financing, keeping the discount rate at 10%.

05

Sensitivity Confirms No Margin of Safety

The full 9×5 sensitivity matrix spans WACC from 8%–12% and TGR from 1.5%–3.5%. The highest implied price in any cell is $24.10. To justify $31.25, one would need a WACC below 8% or a terminal growth assumption above what is realistic for a mature restaurant chain.

06

Strong Operational Quality — Valuation is the Problem

This is not a story about a bad business. EBITDA margins have expanded from 14% in 2021 to ~19% in 2025, FCF generation is strong and growing, and ROIC is ~52%. The issue is purely one of price paid — the market demands a premium that DCF fundamentals cannot validate.

Methodology
Data Source
SEC EDGAR 10-K filings (2016–2025) pulled programmatically via Python. Revenue trend modeled with OLS regression (R² = 0.991, p < 0.001).
Valuation Framework
5-year explicit FCF forecast discounted at WACC (10%). Terminal value blended 50/50 between Gordon Growth Model and 12× EBITDA exit multiple.
Scenario Construction
Three scenarios built by varying revenue growth rates (decelerating to 2.5% terminal) and EBITDA margin assumptions around the base case. WACC held constant.
Sensitivity
9×5 WACC × TGR sensitivity grid (WACC: 8%–12%, TGR: 1.5%–3.5%) run on the base scenario to test robustness of the central estimate.
